Worldwide Fertility Trends and Malaysia’s Story

How Does Fertility Vary Across the World in 2023?

Countries in Africa exhibit much higher fertility rates compared to developed nations like the United States, Australia, and China. The map allows us to see global trends and identify regions where population growth is most significant.

Fertility Rate Trends Across Eight Nations (1960-2022)

Fertility rates in Australia and Canada steadily declined with modernization, while Madagascar and Niger maintain much higher fertility rates with slower declines, indicating persistent traditional family structures and limited access to healthcare.

Does Economic Growth Affect Fertility Rates?

Countries with higher GDPs such as Singapore and Australia tend to have lower fertility rates, whereas countries like Yemen with lower GDP have higher fertility rates.

How long did it take for fertility to fall from more than 6 children to fewer than 2 children per woman?

Malaysia took 59 years to make this transition, while South Korea achieved it in just 24 years, driven by an aggressive birth control policy from the 1960s to the mid-1990s. In contrast, Malaysia’s slower transition was likely due to cultural factors and slower economic growth.

How Does Fertility Rate Vary by Age in Malaysia?

The most significant decline can be seen in the 20-24 age group. These trends reflect Malaysia's transition towards lower fertility rates and smaller family sizes, typical of developing economies.

How Fertility Rates Change Across Malaysia Over Two Decades?

Kelantan has the most significant decline, but remains the second highest fertility rates in 2022, indicating rural family dynamics. Pulau Pinang and W.P. Kuala Lumpur have the lowest fertility rates in 2022. This is likely due to urbanization, higher living costs, and delayed marriages.
